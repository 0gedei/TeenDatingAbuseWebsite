Introduction

Teen dating abuse is a serious issue. Understanding the risk factors and statistics can help identify and prevent abuse.

Risk Factors

  • Age and Development: Teens are learning about relationships and may not recognize the signs of abuse.
  • Peer Pressure: The desire to fit in with peers can lead to accepting unhealthy behaviors.
  • Substance Abuse: Use of drugs or alcohol can impair judgment and increase vulnerability to abuse.
  • Previous Victimization: Teens who have been previously abused or witnessed abuse may be more likely to experience it again.

Statistics

According to the CDC, 1 in 3 teens in the U.S. is a victim of physical, sexual, emotional, or verbal abuse from a dating partner.

Most victims of teen dating violence are between the ages of 12 and 18. While both males and females can be victims, females are more likely to experience severe forms of abuse.
